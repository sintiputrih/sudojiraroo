The Meaning Behind The Song: Nice For What (Remix) by Lauryn Hill

When Lauryn Hill performed her rendition of Drake’s hit song “Nice For What” during a concert at New York’s Apollo Theater in 2018, she added her own personal touch and breathed new life into the track. The remix sampled Hill’s own song, “Ex-Factor,” and showcased her incredible talent as a rapper and singer.

Lauryn Hill’s version of “Nice For What” carries a powerful message that seems to be directed towards a certain individual or group of people who she believes have wronged her. In the opening lines, Hill sings:

“So stop actin’ like you didn’t grow up singing my songs, I stoop down on this bitch like I had wings on my own.”

These lyrics suggest a sense of betrayal and frustration. It is speculated that this verse may be aimed at her former Fugees bandmate, Wyclef Jean, as their strained relationship has been well-documented over the years. Hill had previously lied to Jean about the paternity of her child, which may have contributed to the animosity between them.

While the exact meaning behind the lyrics may be open to interpretation, the passion and emotion behind Hill’s delivery cannot be denied. Her unique blend of rap and soul creates a captivating sound that draws the listener in and encourages further exploration of the song’s deeper meanings.

Personally, this song holds a special place in my heart. As a fan of both Lauryn Hill and Drake, hearing their collaboration was a pleasant surprise. It reminded me of the power of music and how it can bring artists from different generations and genres together.

“Nice For What (Remix)” also serves as a reminder of Hill’s immense talent. Despite taking a step back from the spotlight in recent years, her performance showcases her lyrical prowess and unmatched stage presence. Her ability to effortlessly switch between singing and rapping is nothing short of awe-inspiring.

This song also brings back memories of the original “Ex-Factor” and its impact on the music industry. Hill’s soulful vocals and personal lyrics resonated with audiences and solidified her as a groundbreaking artist. The fact that Drake chose to sample this particular song speaks volumes about its enduring influence.

Furthermore, the collaboration between Hill and Drake highlights the importance of paying homage to the artists who came before us. By sampling “Ex-Factor,” Drake not only introduced a new generation to Hill’s music but also acknowledged her as a trailblazer in the industry.

In conclusion, Lauryn Hill’s remix of “Nice For What” is a testament to her artistry and legacy. Through her powerful lyrics and mesmerizing performance, she demonstrates her ability to captivate audiences and leave a lasting impact. This song serves as a reminder of the timeless nature of music and the way it can connect people across generations and genres.
