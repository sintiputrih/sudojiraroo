Jon Rahm Career

As of September 2021, Rahm has 13 professional wins to his name (6 on the PGA Tour, 5 on the European Tour, and 2 on the Challenge Tour).
He has been ranked as the world number one golfer in the Official World Golf Ranking multiple times, including a 60-week streak from July 2020 to May 2022.

Rahm has finished in the top 10 of major championships on several occasions, including a win at the 2021 U.S. Open, a runner-up finish at the 2021 PGA Championship, and a third-place finish at the 2020 PGA Championship.

In his first four years as a professional, Rahm earned over $27 million in official earnings on the PGA Tour alone.
Rahm’s lowest round on the PGA Tour is a 61, which he achieved twice in 2020.

He also holds the record for the lowest 36-hole score in U.S. Open history, with a score of 9-under par in 2021.

In 2021, Rahm became the first player in PGA Tour history to sink a hole-in-one on a par-4 hole during a tournament.
