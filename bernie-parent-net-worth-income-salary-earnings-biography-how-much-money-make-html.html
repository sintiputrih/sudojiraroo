Bernard Marcel Parent (born April 3, 1945) is a retired Canadian professional ice hockey goaltender who played 13 seasons in the National Hockey League (NHL) with the Philadelphia Flyers, Boston Bruins, and Toronto Maple Leafs, as well as one season in the World Hockey Association (WHA) with the Philadelphia Blazers. He also played one season in the World Hockey League (WHA) with the Philadelphia Blazers. During the 1973–74 and 1974–75 seasons, the Flyers won two Stanley Cups and Parent was awarded the Vezina Trophy and the Conn Smythe Trophy in each of those seasons, which many believe to be the best consecutive seasons by a goalie in NHL history. During that two-year period of dominance, Parent amassed a total of 30 shutouts in regular season and postseason action. Parent, who was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1984, was ranked 63rd on The Hockey News’ list of the Top 100 NHL Players of All-Time in 1998, according to the publication. Parent is still regarded as a Philadelphia institution, more than three decades after he retired from professional baseball.
