Inside Sister Wives star Meri Browns bed and breakfast Lizzies Heritage Inn featuring four bed

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, March 29, 2024

SISTER Wives star Meri Brown’s Utah bed and breakfast, Lizzie’s Heritage Inn, features four bedrooms and a keepsake of her ex Kody, The U.S. Sun has exclusively learned.

The reality star, 52, moved into her bed and breakfast in April with help from Kody, The U.S. Sun reported at the time.

Now, new pics from inside Lizzie's Heritage Inn, exclusively obtained by The U.S. Sun, show never-before-seen treasures and a glimpse into the life of Meri Brown.

The inn, located in the small town of Parowan, Utah, has four bedrooms that can be reserved at a rate of $250 or $300 per night plus tax.

The four rooms are called the Bee Room, the East Room, the Grandma Room, and Sarah's Room.

Sarah's Room is named after Sarah Adams, the founding mother who built the inn in 1870 along with her husband Charles, both of whom are Meri's great great grandparents.

Each room has a king or queen size bed and comes complete with a private bathroom, cable TV, and a welcome tray with water bottles, cookies, and pamphlets on the town of Parowan.

For Sister Wives fans, the best features of each room are the keepsakes from Meri's family passed on from generation to generation.

These include portraits of Meri's loved ones and family members who had passed, dresses that were worn by the TLC star's relatives and old-time knickknacks placed inside each room.

Other interesting finds throughout the home include a pillow in the living room with a photo of Meri and her mom Bonnie, an old piano, and a sign outside that has "The Final Five" couples - one of which points to the right and says, "Meri & Kody 309 mi."

There is also the "Wall of The Dead" where photos are hung of Bonnie (Meri's mom who died in March 2021), Joyce (Meri's grandma), Lizzie (Meri's great grandma), and Sarah.

TOURIST ATTRACTION

Lizzie's Heritage Inn has turned into a tourist attraction.

The U.S. Sun has learned that fans who aren't guests sometimes try entering the home to see their favorite Sister Wives star.

Because of this, the house is secured with outdoor cameras and extra locks around the home.

Fans who are guests of the inn can enjoy the hospitality of the historic home that is surrounded by views of Utah's Valentine Peak and Brian Head Resort.

There is a small gift shop where guests can purchase Lizzie's Heritage Inn merchandise, such as kitchen towels, aprons, coffee mugs, drink coasters, and baseball caps.

Each morning, guests can enjoy the comfort of the welcoming kitchen with breakfast made by a private chef.

Plus, complimentary coffee and tea is available throughout the day.

Upon checking out, guests can leave a review about their stay by signing the guest book.

MERI'S 'RARELY' THERE

Guests of the inn are greeted by Meri's best friend Jenn Sullivan, who according to a source is "tight-lipped about her pal's whereabouts."

Fans of the reality show might be disappointed to learn that the California native might not be at the inn upon arriving.

The U.S. Sun previously reported Meri rarely spends time at the property.

One insider told The U.S. Sun: "Meri isn't at Lizzie's Heritage Inn as much as people think.

"She travels a lot, she's always traveling."

The source went on to question whether the TV personality tricks her followers into thinking that she's at the inn by sharing pics of the property or footage making it appear like she is there, but in reality, she's not.

Another insider revealed: "Meri hasn't been at the inn in quite some time. It's hard to believe she's permanently moved in."

One other local insider revealed that the TV personality is not so known around the town of Parowan because she's not a frequent visitor at some of the main spots downtown.

NEXT CHAPTER

Those who follow Meri on social media might have picked up on the fact that she normally hangs out with her friends and rarely hangs out with the other sister wives - Christine, Robyn, or Janelle.

Four months after announcing her split from the family's patriarch, the reality star moved from Arizona to Utah with help from Kody.

At the end of April, Meri and Kody were spotted by fans at a gas stop picking up food along the way to Utah.

The duo was driving a large moving truck, per a Reddit post, and then seen at Meri's Lizzie's Heritage Inn moving furniture and a piano.

According to another Reddit post, a camera crew filmed the entire move.

A source told The U.S. Sun: "She's done having anything to do with Arizona.

"She's moving on. Kody has been helpful with her move and supportive of her decision."

Around the same time, The U.S. Sun had reported that her four-bedroom, four-bath home that she was renting in Flagstaff for nearly four years was put up for rent.

Located close to the downtown area, the 4,000 square-foot house - that costs over $1million - could be rented for $4,500 per month.
