Louie Vito Wiki Biography

Born Louis Vito on the 20th March 1988 in Columbus, Ohio USA, he is a professional snowboarder who gained recognition at the age of 17 by winning the Australian Open Snowboarding Championships with a backside 1080 trick. Since then, he has become a prominent figure in snowboarding, achieving victory at Winter X Games Europe and US Grand Prix, and securing gold medals in the Winter Drew Tour.

As of mid-2017, it has been estimated that Vito’s net worth is around $2 million, derived from his successful snowboarding career which began in the mid-2000s.

Louie Vito’s Net Worth: $2 Million

During his early years, Vito’s passion for snowboarding led him to move from his hometown of Columbus to Vermont, where he attended the Stratton Mountain School, specializing in skiing and snowboarding. There, he honed his skills and diversified his snowboarding repertoire, dedicating himself to studying, practicing, and competing in events.

His breakthrough came in 2005 at the Australian Open, part of the Burton Global Open Series, where he clinched the gold medal in the Superpipe event. Though he didn’t replicate this success in the Burton Global Open Series, he did win five silver and two bronze medals, significantly boosting his wealth.

He transitioned to different competitions and achieved his first major success at the US Snowboarding Grand Prix in 2007, claiming three silver medals in the superpipe event. The following year, Vito secured his first gold medal at the US Snowboarding Grand Prix in Killington. Continuing his triumph, he won gold medals at Cooper Mountain and Tamarack Mountain in the same event. He maintained his dominance by securing gold medals in subsequent years, further elevating his wealth.

Vito also excelled on the Winter Drew Tour, winning the tour in 2011 and 2012, along with earning three additional gold medals at tour events. Despite limited success in Aspen at the Winter X Games, he fared better at the Winter X Games Europe, securing gold and silver medals in various years.

In 2010, Vito represented the USA national team at the Winter Olympics in Vancouver and placed fifth in the halfpipe event. Additionally, he gained public attention in 2009 when he participated in the reality TV dance competition show “Dancing with the Stars”, partnered with Chelsie Hightower.

Outside of his snowboarding career, Vito is known for his philanthropy, organizing the “Louie Vito Rail Jam” charity event to support aspiring professional riders and contribute to the St. Vincent De Paul charity.
