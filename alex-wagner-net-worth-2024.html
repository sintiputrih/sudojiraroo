Early Life and Education

Alex Wagner was born in Washington, D.C., to a family of mixed heritage. Her father, Carl Wagner, was a prominent political consultant, while her mother, Tin Swe Thant, is an immigrant from Burma. Wagner’s early exposure to politics and international affairs undoubtedly influenced her career choice.

Career Beginnings

Wagner began her career as a cultural correspondent for the Center for American Progress. She later moved on to work for Fader Magazine as the editor-in-chief. Her work in journalism began to gain recognition, and she soon found herself working for prominent news outlets such as MSNBC and CBS.

MSNBC Career

Wagner’s career took a significant leap when she joined MSNBC in 2010. She hosted the daytime program “Now with Alex Wagner” until 2015. Her insightful political commentary and engaging interviewing style made her a favorite among viewers.

CBS and Showtime

After leaving MSNBC, Wagner joined CBS News as a co-host of “CBS This Morning: Saturday”. She also became a senior editor at The Atlantic and started hosting “The Circus” on Showtime, a political documentary series.

Authorial Success

In addition to her journalism career, Wagner has also found success as an author. Her book, “FutureFace: A Family Mystery, an Epic Quest, and the Secret to Belonging”, was published in 2018 and received critical acclaim.

Personal Life

Wagner is married to Sam Kass, a former White House chef and senior policy advisor for nutrition policy in the Obama administration. They have one child together.
