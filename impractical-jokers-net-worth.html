Impractical Jokers Net Worth: Career &amp; Facts

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, January 16, 2024

As of December 2023, the combined net worth of Impractical Jokers is $20 million, making them worth $5 million each.

Impractical Jokers is an American reality prank show with some improvisational elements.

The reality show was produced by NorthSouth Productions, which premiered on truTV on December 15, 2011.

The show occasionally airs on the TBS network.

It starred Joe Gatto, Sal Vulcano, James Murray, and Brian Quinn.

At first, starting a group called The Tenderloins, these four guys use their on-screen chemistry with each other and their humor to make everything interesting for their fans.

One of the ways their admiration spiked was when the group participated in NBC’s reality show competition, ‘It’s Your Show.’ They won the grand prize and a $100,000 winning.

The ninth season of the show premiered on February 4, 2021. Similarly, a full-length film began production in April 2018 and was released on February 21, 2020.

Additionally, there are tons of international versions of the show- British, Dutch, Greek, Lebanese, and Swedish, proving that the fan following of the show is enormous.

Similarly, several spinoffs of the show have been released.

Impractical Jokers and Movie

Plot

Impractical Jokers is a hidden camera reality prank show in which a typical episode is a series of games of dares.

The “Joker” receives a thumbs up or thumbs down for his daring performance.

At the show’s end, the Joker with the most thumbs down is a loser and thus subjected to a “punishment.”

One Joker is challenged to embarrass himself, engaging with the general public as part of the punishment, receiving commands from the fellow jokers who are surveilling the scenario from behind the show.

The Joker will lose a challenge as soon as he refuses to follow any instructions given by the other three Jokers.

Furthermore, the show has been well received by critics.

In fact, Linda Stasi of the New York Post has called the show the most ridiculous show she’s ever seen and possibly the funniest.

The show has been compared to hit prank shows, including Jackass and Candid Camera. In fact, the critics have offered praise for its unique twist on the genre.

Similarly, the series garnered 1.5 million viewers during its premiere on December 15, 2011.

Impractical Jokers: Development

After winning a grand prize of $100,000 in NBC’s “It’s Your Show,” the group went into television.

They filmed a pilot episode for a scripted sitcom for Spike TV in 2008. However, the show did not go to series.

On April 12, 2011, TruTV announced Impractical Jokers, which was initially programmed to be named Mission: Uncomfortable, eight months before the show’s debut.

However, James Murray explained the concept of a hidden camera.

Two of the jokers, Vulcano and Quinn, gave their pilot episode to TruTV, which was recorded on their iPhones.

Impractical Jokers: Movie

The truTV made an announcement on March 7, 2018, that Impractical Jokers was renewed for the eighth season and also a feature film.

Moreover, they also announced that the film would be directed by Chris Henchy and produced by Funny or Die.

The film’s production started at the end of April 2018. Similarly, the trailer of the film was released on December 17, 2019.

Ultimately, the film was released on February 21, 2020.

The film grossed $10.7 million at the worldwide box office collection on a budget of $3 million.

Career

Joe Gatto worked as a salesman at the baby retail store, Giggle, and was a car salesman at a Nissan dealership.

Similarly, Joe also wrote and starred in the TV movie The Tenderloins in 2009. Besides, in 2014 he appeared on the TV series Jokers Wild.

Brian “Q” Quinn worked as a ride operator at Beachland Amusements, a video clerk at Blockbuster, and a fireman for the FDNY before the show aired.

Similarly, he has also appeared in several movies, including 1996 Warshots, 1999 Dogma, 1999 Bg Helium Dog, and 2000 Vulgar.

James Murray sold golf balls at a golf course and then worked for NorthSouth Productions, which has produced shows for truTV, AT&T, TLC, MTV, etc.

He acted in a student film called Private Parts and Judas in the movie Damned!

Similarly, Murray was a director, editor, and producer of the 2002 short film Subway.

Likewise, he also wrote, directed, and produced the 2005 short film Superdude & Friend, Criss-Cross, and Standups in Love.

Sal Vulcano worked as a pizza delivery guy, as a bartender at The Full Cup, and has performed standup comedy. He has acted in the movies Damned! and Just Another Romantic Wrestling Comedy.

Vulcano was involved in the management of bat from 2010 to 2014 in Staten Island named The Full Cup.
