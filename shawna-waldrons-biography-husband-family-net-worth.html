Who is Shawna Waldron?

Shawna Waldron is an American actress, producer, and writer, best known to the world as Becky O’Shea in the comedy film “Little Giants” (1994), and also as Bonnie Stiles in the TV series “Ladies Man”, among numerous other projects on which she has worked so far in her career.

Wiki: Age, Childhood, and Education

Born Shawna Langill Waldron on the 25th January 1982 in Glendale, California USA; from an early age, Shawna started showing interest in performing arts, particularly acting and did all to become a professional actress as soon as she could.

This happened in 1994 when Shawna was 12 years old.

Immediate Success

The role of Becky O’Shea in the sports comedy film “Little Giants” was Shawna’s debut, but it immediately launched her to stardom. Following her initial success, Shawna was chosen for the part of Lucy Shepherd, the daughter of Michael Douglas’ character in the film “The American President” in 1995.

Before the end of the ‘90s, Shawna continued with popular roles, firstly as Sarah Marshall in the television film “A Change of Heart”, and then as Anne Harrison in the TV comedy series “Family Rules” (1999). Also, she secured the recurring role of Bonnie Stiles in yet another comedy series, “Ladies Man” (1999-2000). These were the roles that celebrated her as an actress but also started showing her transition into adult roles.

The 2000s and 2010s

Shawna started the 2000s with the lead role in the horror-thriller film “The Yellow Sign” (2001), starring next to Dale Snowberger and David Reynolds, after which she was cast as Tess in the television film “A Long Way Home” in 2003. She didn’t have much success in the 2000s as an actress, making it hard for her to continue her career, however, she was Azalea in the television film “Poison Ivy: The Secret Society” in 2008, and Amanda in the drama film “3 Days in L.A.” in 2009.

Since 2010, Shawna hasn’t been quite so active as an actress, but still made a few notable appearances. She was Chloe in the horror crime-mystery film “Cyrus” in 2010, starring Tiffany Shepis, Max Lesser, and Brooke Parker, and then in 2012 appeared in the horror film “Lizzie”, and the following year in the action comedy film “Police Guys”, with Jon Lee Brody, Stephen Kramer Glickman, and Lester Speight.

The same year she starred next to another child star, Edward Furlong in the horror film “Stitch”, and in 2014 had the lead role in the drama film “Night Lights”, accompanied by Stephen Louis Grush and Kate Black-Spence.

Most Recent Projects

Shawna took a hiatus from acting which lasted from 2014 until 2018, when she returned with the role of Clara in the drama film “Dead Man Rising”, starring Sean Anthony Moran, Travis Johns, and Corbin Bernsen, while most recently she appeared in the drama film “Teacher” (2019), with David Dastmalchian, Kevin Pollak, and Curtis Edward Jackson, as the main stars of the film.

Writer and Producer

Shawna has expressed herself creatively in other ways than acting, as a writer and producer. Back in 2004 she extensively worked on the film “To Kill a Mockumentary”, with Stephen Wallis, who was also the director of the film, and which starred Jason London, Brian Krause, and Edie McClurg, and in 2009, she produced the documentary “Char·ac·ter”.
